Opportunity cost is a fundamental concept in economics that applies broadly to personal decision-making. Every choice to pursue one path necessarily means foregoing alternatives. Understanding this trade-off is essential for making informed decisions about how to allocate limited resources, including time and attention.

The notion that intelligence cannot be measured by years of experience alone has some empirical support. Research on expertise development shows that deliberate practice quality matters more than raw duration. A person who practices intentionally for two years may outperform someone with ten years of passive experience in the same domain.

Starting earlier generally provides advantages in compounding returns, whether in financial investment or skill development. However, the literature also suggests that late starts can be compensated through focused intensity and strategic prioritization of high-leverage activities.
